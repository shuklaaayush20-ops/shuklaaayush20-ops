# Case Study: UPI Fraud via Online Marketplace

Online Payment (UPI) Fraud occurs when scammers manipulate victims into transferring money via the Unified Payments Interface using deceptive tactics. College students are frequent targets because they regularly browse platforms like OLX or Facebook Marketplace for budget-friendly deals.

In this realistic scenario, a college student finds a listing for a high-quality, second-hand electric guitar at a surprisingly low price. Excited by the deal, the student contacts the seller. The seller, sounding highly professional, agrees to the price but claims they need a small token advance to "reserve" the instrument due to high demand from other buyers.

The step-by-step manipulation begins here: The scammer sends a QR code or a "Receive Money" request on a UPI app (like Google Pay or PhonePe). They falsely instruct the student to enter their UPI PIN, claiming it is required to "receive a refund for a test transaction" or to "verify their account status." Because the student is eager to secure the guitar, they ignore the app's standard warning that entering a PIN *sends* money. They enter the PIN, and the funds are instantly deducted from their bank account. The scammer immediately blocks the student's number and deletes the listing.

The consequences are severe for college students living on a tight budget. Beyond the immediate financial loss, the student experiences immense stress, feelings of vulnerability, and a lasting loss of trust in digital marketplaces.
